Factors Affecting Survival¶

I have the impression that rate of survival might have been affected by the socio economic status of passengers, that is, those who were in the first class might have got the greatest chance of survival compared to the other classes. Those who were in the highest class might have received a great care, or given life jackets compared to others. Moreover, I assume priority might have been given to children and women.

Since my interest is to find out What factors made people more likely to survive, I would like to explore the frequency table of survived passengers (taking Survived as a dependent variable) against passengers gender, their class and Age (three independent variables). For the analysis, I will also consider all the three independent variables as categorical variables (Sex, Pclass and Age goups will be taken).

To test if there is association or some sort of relation between these variables, I will consider contigency table, Conditional Percents (row percents and columns percents). By conditional percents I mean, my conclusions will be based on the following idea.

Two categorical variables are related (associated) if at least two rows noticeably differ in the pattern of row percents, and if at least two columns noticeably differ in the pattern of columns percents.

Inorder to test the significance of the contingency tables, test statistic Chi-square will be taken.

Chi-square test

Test Hypothesis: I will conduct chi-square test to determine the relationship between variables 'Survived' and 'Passengers Class'.

HO: there is no association between Survived and Passengers class (population mean of Survived is same as population mean of Pclass)
H1: there is association between Survived and Passengers class (there is difference in the population mean of Survived and Pclass)

Conclusions¶

Using contingency table (chi-square test), it is proved that there is association between Survived and the three factors (Pclass, Gender and Age). However, this doesn't imply that we should ignore other factors that might have an impact either between or with in the different variables. It should also be noted that chi-square test does not give us much information about the strength of the relationship of the variables or its substantive significance in the population. For example, I discovered that survival is not distributed similarly across the different Classes of passengers. Based on the results we can't measure the strength of the relationship between the three different classes.For that matter chi-square together with measures of association like lambda, Cramer's V or gamma needs to be done to guide us in deciding whether a relationship is important and worth pursuing.
